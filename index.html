Orbus the Bereft — Complete Mythology
OriginAt the beginning there were thirteen godlike beings. One father. Twelve sons. Orbus was the thirteenth. His nature was chaos — not by choice but by what he was. He sowed madness among his brothers. He turned his chaos toward his father and would have killed him. His brothers and father combined and sealed him inside a comet and sent him here. He did not choose to come to Earth. He was thrown here.
ArrivalHe arrived with the Younger Dryas comet impact approximately 12,900 years ago. The impact nearly destroyed Earth. Ended the ice age. Killed the megafauna. Destroyed civilizations that existed before recorded history. When he arrived he was still strong. He bent the survivors to his will. Demanded the blood of their brightest children.
What He Is NowWeakened over thirteen thousand years. He hides in the seams of things. The edges of firelight. The dark between stars. He lies dormant for centuries and surfaces when the chosen one’s flame burns bright enough to pull him up.
His CycleWhen he surfaces he feeds on the Kindled — the bright-blooded — through the Fenton bloodline. Gets to functional strength. Then focuses entirely on the chosen one. Tries to inhabit them completely. Has failed twice. This is the third attempt.
His MotiveHe believes if he inhabits the chosen one completely he can use their power to nudge a comet — NASA confirms a tiny push is all it takes — and the impact will thin the seams between this world and his father’s world enough for him to step through and go home. Whether Earth survives is not his concern.
What the Legends SayThe ancient traditions — including the medicine woman’s — say yes the comet thins the seams. But what steps through is not a god going home. It is something coming in. Orbus has never addressed this. He only knows what he knows.
His NatureHe is the Bereft. Not because he chose evil. Because thirteen thousand years of isolation and hunger and diminishment has made him monstrous. He was cast out because his nature was chaos. The isolation turned the chaos inward. He is the man on the street corner with newspapers taped to his body counting things in the air that nobody else can see. Completely certain. Completely alone. Completely lost. He was not always this.
The DelusionHe has been sitting in the dark for thirteen thousand years telling himself a story. A reason. A purpose. He believes if he gets home his brothers and his father will be waiting. That the impact will thin the seams. That something will be on the other side. The reader gradually sees what Anthony and Nicole cannot yet see — the twelve gods are not waiting for him. The way home may not exist the way he believes it does. He does not consider this. He has never considered this. He only knows what he knows. And what he knows is the story he has been telling himself in the dark for thirteen thousand years.
His AppearanceFeatureless. Plain. A face the eye cannot hold. Nothing to name, nothing to keep. Only the eyes are distinct — burning, half filled with sorrow, half filled with something older than hate. A crown of light appears above his head near the gifted. Not a symbol of power. A wound that never healed taking the shape of what he once thought he deserved. He is the king of madness and loneliness.
As He FeedsHis featureless face flickers. Not becoming something. Just flickering like a candle in wind. In those flickers — faces. Young faces. Adolescents. Children. Even babies. The faces of everyone he has ever consumed passing across his featureless face like reflections on disturbed water. He is not haunted by them. They are simply part of what he is now. Cole Fenton sees this when he is close enough. Sometimes in the flickering he sees faces he recognizes.
Ancient DepictionsThe Urfa Man — oldest life-size statue ever found, 9000 BC, no mouth, black obsidian eyes. Found near Göbekli Tepe. The survivors made a statue of him. The Sphinx — original form was a wolf, built during the wet Sahara period after his arrival, facing east. Pillar 43 at Göbekli Tepe — the vulture stone — depicts the Younger Dryas comet impact date. Other pillars depict a featureless figure holding a child. The wolf stone depicts a wolf-man figure. In the ancient traditions he is sometimes called the Lonely Wolf — the figure that moves through the dark between the fires.

The Book — The Self-Writing Record
OriginMade by the medicine man at Göbekli Tepe approximately 9600 BC. He was the most holy person his people had ever produced. He spent three days fashioning it from a fragment of the comet that brought Orbus — black obsidian on one side, dried skin on the other, bound with sinew cord. The impact debris still faintly warm, humming at a frequency felt in the back teeth. Animals would not approach it. He wove his medicine into the binding using rituals that died with him. When it was complete he breathed white light from his body into the front cover and pressed his hands in. His skin burned and sizzled. He did not stop. No living tradition knows what he did those three days. Only what he made.
What It DoesThe book writes itself. It records the chosen ones — their story, what happened to them, what Orbus did, what it cost — in the language of each era it records. Nobody writes in it. The entries simply appear. Each section in the language closest to the chosen one it describes.
How It MovesThe book moves to the chosen one when the time comes. The medicine woman’s tradition — a line of medicine women stretching back to the survivors of the Younger Dryas — carries it forward. Grandmother to granddaughter. With specific instructions: when the comet burns in October, find where the fire is. Get there before the other one does. The hibachi chef is the current medicine woman in this line. She sent the book to the household after seeing Carter put his hands in the flame. She was recognizing, not discovering.
How It HelpsIt shows the chosen one what came before. Every chosen one who ever lived. Their story already written. So they know they are not the first. So they know the pattern. So they know what Orbus is doing before he finishes doing it.
ArrivalThe book arrived addressed to Carter. Carter is eighteen months old. The hibachi chef — a medicine woman whose tradition has known about the book for generations — found it and sent it to the household when she saw Carter put his hands in the flame and the crown appeared above his shadow. She knew the time had come.
ContentsApproximately thirteen sections. Each in a different language. The front section is Göbekli Tepe proto-script — the oldest written language on Earth. Nicole eventually identifies it and gets partial translation through an archaeologist contact. Middle sections in various ancient and medieval languages. One section in archaic English that Anthony can barely parse — this is Michaela Marie’s section, the one he finds when he flips through at midnight. The last pages — mostly blank. In the bottom corner of the final page: Carter’s name and birthday. October 13 2024. Written in no hand that belongs to anyone.
Ancient Sites — The Survivors’ Monuments
Göbekli Tepe — 9600–8200 BCSoutheastern Turkey. The oldest temple ever found. Pre-dates any other megalithic site by 6,000 years. Deliberately buried by its builders. T-shaped pillars with animal carvings. Pillar 43 — the vulture stone — encodes the date of the Younger Dryas impact as a star map. The first written word found here means God. LIDAR has detected 15–20 additional structures beneath the hill not yet excavated. One pillar depicts a featureless figure holding a child. One pillar has a wolf carved into it. The survivors built this to warn whoever came after them.
Karahan Tepe — Same Period45 kilometers east of Göbekli Tepe. Sister site. 250 obelisks. A human face carved emerging from bedrock on a serpent-like neck. Deliberately buried. LIDAR revealing T-shaped pillars inside a ritual well. In 1999 during early excavations something was disturbed. The Fenton bloodline activity in Nicole’s research spikes noticeably in the early 2000s.
The Urfa Man — 9000 BCFound near Göbekli Tepe. Oldest life-size human statue ever found. No mouth. Eyes originally emphasized by black obsidian sunk into deep holes. The obsidian comes from a geological formation buried under the Younger Dryas impact debris. The survivors used a piece of what brought him here to make his eyes.
The Sphinx — 10,000–12,000 BCBuilt during the wet Sahara period after the impact. Original form was a wolf — not a lion, not a human face. The head was recarved by later civilizations who found it and didn’t understand what they were looking at. Faces east. Watching the sky where the comet came from. Dr. Robert Schoch’s water erosion evidence dates it to this period.
Tell Qaramel — Syria — 10,500 BCFive stone towers. The earliest structures built by the survivors. Before the full theology developed. Just towers watching the sky.

It is the ability to be completely present with something in its fullest most terrifying form and not flinch. Nobody in thirteen thousand years has ever been fully present with Orbus. Every Chosen before Carter either burned against him or collapsed. Carter goes completely still. Not submission. Not resistance. Presence. He holds the full weight of thirteen thousand years of Orbus’s grief and loneliness and does not break. And in that moment — Carter’s eyes flash ice blue. The father’s light. The cold between stars. The core mechanism is presence, not combat.

Waiting in the bloodline for the one who could hold it.
