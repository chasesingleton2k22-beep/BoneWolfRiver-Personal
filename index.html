Orbus the Bereft — Complete Mythology
OriginAt the beginning there were thirteen godlike beings. One father. Twelve sons. Orbus was the thirteenth. His nature was chaos — not by choice but by what he was. He sowed madness among his brothers. He turned his chaos toward his father and would have killed him. His brothers and father combined and sealed him inside a comet and sent him here. He did not choose to come to Earth. He was thrown here.
ArrivalHe arrived with the Younger Dryas comet impact approximately 12,900 years ago. The impact nearly destroyed Earth. Ended the ice age. Killed the megafauna. Destroyed civilizations that existed before recorded history. When he arrived he was still strong. He bent the survivors to his will. Demanded the blood of their brightest children.
What He Is NowWeakened over thirteen thousand years. He hides in the seams of things. The edges of firelight. The dark between stars. He lies dormant for centuries and surfaces when the chosen one’s flame burns bright enough to pull him up.
His CycleWhen he surfaces he feeds on the Kindled — the bright-blooded — through the Fenton bloodline. Gets to functional strength. Then focuses entirely on the chosen one. Tries to inhabit them completely. Has failed twice. This is the third attempt.
His MotiveHe believes if he inhabits the chosen one completely he can use their power to nudge a comet — NASA confirms a tiny push is all it takes — and the impact will thin the seams between this world and his father’s world enough for him to step through and go home. Whether Earth survives is not his concern.
What the Legends SayThe ancient traditions — including the medicine woman’s — say yes the comet thins the seams. But what steps through is not a god going home. It is something coming in. Orbus has never addressed this. He only knows what he knows.
His NatureHe is the Bereft. Not because he chose evil. Because thirteen thousand years of isolation and hunger and diminishment has made him monstrous. He was cast out because his nature was chaos. The isolation turned the chaos inward. He is the man on the street corner with newspapers taped to his body counting things in the air that nobody else can see. Completely certain. Completely alone. Completely lost. He was not always this.
The DelusionHe has been sitting in the dark for thirteen thousand years telling himself a story. A reason. A purpose. He believes if he gets home his brothers and his father will be waiting. That the impact will thin the seams. That something will be on the other side. The reader gradually sees what Anthony and Nicole cannot yet see — the twelve gods are not waiting for him. The way home may not exist the way he believes it does. He does not consider this. He has never considered this. He only knows what he knows. And what he knows is the story he has been telling himself in the dark for thirteen thousand years.
His AppearanceFeatureless. Plain. A face the eye cannot hold. Nothing to name, nothing to keep. Only the eyes are distinct — burning, half filled with sorrow, half filled with something older than hate. A crown of light appears above his head near the gifted. Not a symbol of power. A wound that never healed taking the shape of what he once thought he deserved. He is the king of madness and loneliness.
As He FeedsHis featureless face flickers. Not becoming something. Just flickering like a candle in wind. In those flickers — faces. Young faces. Adolescents. Children. Even babies. The faces of everyone he has ever consumed passing across his featureless face like reflections on disturbed water. He is not haunted by them. They are simply part of what he is now. Cole Fenton sees this when he is close enough. Sometimes in the flickering he sees faces he recognizes.
Ancient DepictionsThe Urfa Man — oldest life-size statue ever found, 9000 BC, no mouth, black obsidian eyes. Found near Göbekli Tepe. The survivors made a statue of him. The Sphinx — original form was a wolf, built during the wet Sahara period after his arrival, facing east. Pillar 43 at Göbekli Tepe — the vulture stone — depicts the Younger Dryas comet impact date. Other pillars depict a featureless figure holding a child. The wolf stone depicts a wolf-man figure. In the ancient traditions he is sometimes called the Lonely Wolf — the figure that moves through the dark between the fires.
The Two Tiers
Tier One — The KindledChildren up to age thirteen with natural gifts. Charisma, athletic ability, intelligence, the specific quality that makes rooms notice them. The Fenton bloodline hunts these and delivers them. Their essence sustains Orbus during dormancy. The cutoff is thirteen — after that the gift settles into the person and closes.
Tier Two — The ChosenThe vessels. Britannia. Carter Walks-With-Fire. Modern Carter. These carry the actual gift — the Bone Calm. Orbus cultivates them across generations. Every time the gift is used he steps further inside. Eventually they go mad from the combination of grief, his deepening presence, and Fenton acceleration.
What Drives the Chosen Mad — Three Forces
One — The CostSomething beloved is always taken when the gift is used. The grief accumulates.
Two — Orbus DeepeningEvery use of the gift lets him step further inside. The Bone Calm they feel IS him. The voice on the wind Carter always thought was himself — it’s Orbus getting louder.
Three — Fenton AccelerationThe Fenton rituals near a chosen one push Orbus deeper faster. When Eira was taken — a Fenton ritual — it pushed Orbus deeper into Britannia at the exact moment it broke her heart.
The Wolf Connection
The Šúŋka Wa WatchesThe wolf doesn’t serve Orbus. The wolf watches Orbus. The wolf is the alarm system the bloodline developed against him across ten thousand years. Archie watching the empty armchair. The Šúŋka Wa going still on the plains. They see what humans can’t.
The Same Wolf Across TimelinesArchie has one ice blue eye. The Šúŋka Wa has one ice blue eye. These are not descendants. This is the same animal across timelines — sustained through something the medicine woman’s tradition built into the wolf bond at its origin. The wolf is the oldest sentinel in the story. Older than the Book. Older than any of the Chosen. Older than the mythology the survivors built. When the wolf watches the empty armchair it is drawing on something deeper than instinct. It was made for exactly this.
The Isolation RuleOrbus cannot complete the possession while the wolf is present and watching. This is not incidental. The wolf bond was specifically built to prevent it. Everything the Fenton bloodline does is designed ultimately to separate Carter from Archie. Every scene where Archie is not in frame becomes tense in a completely new way once this rule is established. The reader knows. Carter does not.
The Oldest DepictionIn the oldest legends — before any civilization had a name for him — Orbus is described as a giant wolf stalking the world alone in the dark between things. Not because he is a wolf. Because the survivors reached for the most powerful image they had for something vast, lonely, and moving through the dark. The Lonely Wolf. The Lost Wolf. Carter walking with the Šúŋka Wa is the inversion of this — the Chosen walks with the wolf while Orbus is the wolf walking alone.

The Book — The Self-Writing Record
OriginMade by the medicine man at Göbekli Tepe approximately 9600 BC. He was the most holy person his people had ever produced. He spent three days fashioning it from a fragment of the comet that brought Orbus — black obsidian on one side, dried skin on the other, bound with sinew cord. The impact debris still faintly warm, humming at a frequency felt in the back teeth. Animals would not approach it. He wove his medicine into the binding using rituals that died with him. When it was complete he breathed white light from his body into the front cover and pressed his hands in. His skin burned and sizzled. He did not stop. No living tradition knows what he did those three days. Only what he made.
What It DoesThe book writes itself. It records the chosen ones — their story, what happened to them, what Orbus did, what it cost — in the language of each era it records. Nobody writes in it. The entries simply appear. Each section in the language closest to the chosen one it describes.
How It MovesThe book moves to the chosen one when the time comes. The medicine woman’s tradition — a line of medicine women stretching back to the survivors of the Younger Dryas — carries it forward. Grandmother to granddaughter. With specific instructions: when the comet burns in October, find where the fire is. Get there before the other one does. The hibachi chef is the current medicine woman in this line. She sent the book to the household after seeing Carter put his hands in the flame. She was recognizing, not discovering.
How It HelpsIt shows the chosen one what came before. Every chosen one who ever lived. Their story already written. So they know they are not the first. So they know the pattern. So they know what Orbus is doing before he finishes doing it.
ArrivalThe book arrived addressed to Carter. Carter is eighteen months old. The hibachi chef — a medicine woman whose tradition has known about the book for generations — found it and sent it to the household when she saw Carter put his hands in the flame and the crown appeared above his shadow. She knew the time had come.
ContentsApproximately thirteen sections. Each in a different language. The front section is Göbekli Tepe proto-script — the oldest written language on Earth. Nicole eventually identifies it and gets partial translation through an archaeologist contact. Middle sections in various ancient and medieval languages. One section in archaic English that Anthony can barely parse — this is Michaela Marie’s section, the one he finds when he flips through at midnight. The last pages — mostly blank. In the bottom corner of the final page: Carter’s name and birthday. October 13 2024. Written in no hand that belongs to anyone.
Ancient Sites — The Survivors’ Monuments
Göbekli Tepe — 9600–8200 BCSoutheastern Turkey. The oldest temple ever found. Pre-dates any other megalithic site by 6,000 years. Deliberately buried by its builders. T-shaped pillars with animal carvings. Pillar 43 — the vulture stone — encodes the date of the Younger Dryas impact as a star map. The first written word found here means God. LIDAR has detected 15–20 additional structures beneath the hill not yet excavated. One pillar depicts a featureless figure holding a child. One pillar has a wolf carved into it. The survivors built this to warn whoever came after them.
Karahan Tepe — Same Period45 kilometers east of Göbekli Tepe. Sister site. 250 obelisks. A human face carved emerging from bedrock on a serpent-like neck. Deliberately buried. LIDAR revealing T-shaped pillars inside a ritual well. In 1999 during early excavations something was disturbed. The Fenton bloodline activity in Nicole’s research spikes noticeably in the early 2000s.
The Urfa Man — 9000 BCFound near Göbekli Tepe. Oldest life-size human statue ever found. No mouth. Eyes originally emphasized by black obsidian sunk into deep holes. The obsidian comes from a geological formation buried under the Younger Dryas impact debris. The survivors used a piece of what brought him here to make his eyes.
The Sphinx — 10,000–12,000 BCBuilt during the wet Sahara period after the impact. Original form was a wolf — not a lion, not a human face. The head was recarved by later civilizations who found it and didn’t understand what they were looking at. Faces east. Watching the sky where the comet came from. Dr. Robert Schoch’s water erosion evidence dates it to this period.
Tell Qaramel — Syria — 10,500 BCFive stone towers. The earliest structures built by the survivors. Before the full theology developed. Just towers watching the sky.

The women in Brittany’s bloodline don’t carry the gift. They carry something that makes them see what others can’t. That sensitivity is what Orbus needed in the vessel that would birth the final chosen one.

The RealtorCalled Brittany specifically. She never signed up for any list. Something off about him. She tries to describe his face afterward and can’t. Just the eyes. Something burning in them she files away as intensity.
Brittany as a ChildUsed to walk past the Victorian house on her way to school. Obsessed with it. Specifically the sunroom. Drew pictures of it over and over. Always the chair with the dragon heads. She had never been inside. Never seen the chair up close. Drew it exactly right every time. Orbus nudging her mind from childhood. Not forcing. Just pulling. The way gravity pulls.
Moving InThe chair was already there. In the sunroom. Exactly where she always drew it. She stood in the doorway for a long time. Didn’t tell Anthony.
The Hidden RoomDuring renovation they knock on a wall that sounds hollow. Open it up. Small room. No windows. Sealed from inside. Old wood smell. Something left behind by whoever sealed it. What’s inside is workshopped.
Cole Fenton — Complete
The Bloodline TheologyAppalachian mountain family. The theology passed father to son for generations. They believe Orbus is God. Not a god. God. The one true thing. Every other religion has a version of him. The Fentons are the only ones who have actually met him. Done his work. Firsthand. They call the marked children the Kindled. The fire in those children is wrong fire. The devil’s mark. They believe they are doing holy work by delivering them. Purifying them.
Cole’s OriginCole Fenton’s father took his baby brother away one day. Said he was going to stay with relatives in Texas. Cole never met them. Never heard of them. His brother never came back. Cole knew his father killed him. Spent years waiting to be old enough to do something about it. Then his father sat him down and explained everything. The theology. The Kindled. The holy work. What his brother was. Why it had to happen. Cole made his choice.
Physical DescriptionNot large but takes up space. Pale — not from being indoors but from something older. Dark lank hair pushed back from a face of angles. Jaw and cheekbone and the hollow under the eye of a man who eats when he remembers to and forgets often. Big hands for his frame. Calloused from patient work done in the dark over a long time. Eyes that burned with the demented certainty of divine purpose.
Cole Sees GhostsThe shadows of the children. Not haunting him. Just residue. Little flickering presences at the edge of vision. He sees them in mirrors. Small shapes. The suggestion of a face. He thinks it’s the cost of the holy work. That you carry what you do. He accepts it. But they’re getting more frequent. And lately one of them has been looking back at him.
Nicole’s DiscoveryShe maps the Fenton bloodline across centuries through missing children clusters. Same signature. Same age range — under thirteen. Most gifted child in the community. No body. No explanation. The Fenton name appears in records going back to 1887. The clusters spike when Orbus surfaces. Go dormant when he sleeps. The dots are moving toward Chattanooga.
Story Architecture — Locked
The Ending — LockedCarter does not destroy Orbus. He sees him. The Bone Calm was never a weapon. Every Chosen before Carter used it as one and none of them broke the cycle. The Bone Calm is the ability to be completely present with something in its fullest most terrifying form and not flinch. Nobody has ever been fully present with Orbus. Not in thirteen thousand years. Every Chosen either burned against him or collapsed. Carter goes completely still. Not submission. Not resistance. Presence. He holds the full weight of thirteen thousand years of Orbus’s grief and loneliness and does not break. Being truly witnessed by something that isn’t afraid of what it sees — does to Orbus what thirteen millennia of waiting never could.
Dark EndingOrbus burns away because he finally, briefly, was not alone. That is the only mercy available to something that broken.
Light EndingWhatever Orbus was before the isolation surfaces for a single moment. The chaos freed becomes something else entirely.
Final Image — Both VersionsThe Book closes itself. On the last page new text appears in the original hand. The same script as the very first page. Two words only: He remembered.

His own voice. But with the certainty of a child who has never once second-guessed himself. Anthony cannot make out the words. Just Carter’s profile. Moonlight finding him. The Wetherbee name burned into the back of the chair above his son’s head. Carter is speaking to whatever is in the yard. Then the Bone Calm still. The night shifts slightly — not a shape, not a sound, just the air changing. Carter nods. Like he received an answer. Then he turns and looks directly at Anthony through the glass. He knew Anthony was there the entire time. Carter says one sentence. In his own small four-year-old voice. Something from inside Anthony’s childhood. Something Anthony has never spoken aloud. Not to Brittany. Not to Nicole. Not to anyone. Something buried so completely he forgot the act of burying it. Something Carter has no way to know. Anthony slides down the glass door and sits on the kitchen floor with his back against it. Not going outside. Just sitting in the dark with his face in his hands. Not afraid. Anthony puts his hand on the back of his son’s head. Neither of them says anything. The tears aren’t fear. They’re the specific grief of a man who spent his whole life not trusting inherited things finally understanding that the thing he built a wall against has been standing inside him this whole time. Carter didn’t get it from somewhere else. Carter got it from him. [What Carter says is yours to find. Only you know what is locked in Anthony.]

She has been in that Victorian house since Carter arrived and she is the reason Orbus has not been able to move into Carter the way he moved into the others before. There is already a woman in the room watching the fire. And she was the one who said burn bright my fire at the edge of her life. The question that runs through the modern timeline — is the presence in the house Orbus or Roxy — is the Paranormal Activity engine. Anthony cannot be sure. The reader cannot be sure. Not until the ending.

She married Anthony because of what Carter was going to be. Not arranged. Not sinister. The Book Smells Like Fire — LockedThe package smelled like fire and ash. Not because it came from somewhere burning. Carter Walks-With-Fire held this book in 1876. His hands were in the pages. A century and a half later still in the ink.
The 14th Name — LockedNicole counts thirteen cultures with thirteen names. She does not know the fourteenth. The fourteenth is the name Orbus’s brothers and father used for him before the casting out. Before the chaos. Before any of this. His true name.
